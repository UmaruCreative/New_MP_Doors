Does composite construction require any maintenance? +
No ongoing maintenance is required from the composite frame. The material does not absorb moisture, so it does not rot, warp, or require repainting because of weather cycling. The factory finish is applied to composite substrate — unlike wood, where the substrate beneath the finish is actively expanding and contracting, driving the finish to eventually crack and fail. Composite construction engineered with HydroShield Technology seals all six sides of the door system, including the frame edges and corner joints that are most exposed to water in a front-entry application. [VERIFY: confirm HydroShield Technology is applicable to this specific entry door model and update the language above if not confirmed.]
How does composite construction compare to a steel or wood entry door? +
Steel entry doors are inexpensive and initially strong, but the painted finish on steel is vulnerable to chipping and rust — once the steel surface is exposed, corrosion begins, particularly in coastal, humid, or salt-air environments. Wood doors offer warmth and traditional aesthetics but require periodic repainting, re-sealing, and adjustment as the wood expands and contracts with seasonal moisture changes. Composite construction resists the failure modes specific to both: it does not rust or corrode like steel, and it does not swell, stick, or require refinishing like wood. The factory finish on composite has a stable, non-moving substrate beneath it — which is why it holds.

What should I do if the door arrives with shipping damage? +
Inspect the frame, glass panel, and hardware before the delivery crew leaves the site. Photograph any damage before moving the door. Report delivery damage within 5 calendar days of receipt by calling 1-888-366-7717 or emailing customerservice@mpdoors.com. Damage reported after the 5-day window is not eligible for coverage — that deadline does not extend. Contact MP Doors directly; you will not be asked to return the door to a retail location.
How does the Modern Full Lite compare to the Oval Lite Entry Door? +
Both doors use composite construction and carry the same structural warranty terms. The Modern Full Lite is a full-glass panel — the entire door face is glass, maximizing light and delivering a clean contemporary profile suited to modern and transitional homes. The Oval Lite uses a partial oval glass insert — less glass area, more wall material around the insert, privacy-forward, and suited to traditional, classic, or craftsman home styles. If your home has a modern or contemporary design and light is the priority, the Modern Full Lite is the right choice. If your home is traditional and interior privacy from the street is a priority, the Oval Lite is the better fit.
