The Materials Behind the Glass

01

FULL GLASS PANEL

Top to Bottom. Edge to Edge.

The Full Lite door covers the entire door face with glass — no solid panel area, no partial insert. This is the maximum-light entry door configuration: daylight enters the front of the home across the full door width and carries through the entry into every room connected to it. The White composite frame holds the glass and the geometry without seasonal movement.

02

BLINDS BETWEEN GLASS

Full Light or Full Privacy — Your Call.

The Blinds Between Glass option installs adjustable blinds inside the sealed glass unit — tilt, raise, and lower from the interior without opening the door. Open them fully and the door behaves like a clear full-lite panel. Lower them completely and the entry is fully private. The blinds are sealed inside the glass, which means they are protected from dust, cleaning, and damage. They can't be bent, tangled, or broken by children or pets.

What is the difference between the Full Lite Entry Door and the Modern Full Lite Entry Door? +
Both are full glass panel entry doors. The key differences are finish palette and glass options. The Modern Full Lite is positioned for contemporary composite construction — it emphasizes the composite frame and HydroShield technology and is oriented toward modern homes. The Full Lite comes in White only and adds the Blinds Between Glass option, which gives complete on-demand privacy control that the Modern Full Lite does not list. If built-in adjustable blinds are a priority, the Full Lite is the one to choose. [VERIFY: confirm the full distinction between the two models from the Home Depot listings — particularly whether the Modern Full Lite also offers Blinds Between Glass.]
How do Blinds Between Glass work — are they hard to operate? +
The Blinds Between Glass system operates with a small handle or cord on the interior edge of the glass unit — tilt the slats to control light diffusion, raise or lower the full blind for open or fully private settings. The operation requires no more effort than a standard window blind. Because the blinds are sealed inside the glass unit, they are protected from dust, fingerprints, and physical damage — you never have to clean between the slats and children or pets cannot reach or bend them.

Is this door appropriate for street-facing entries where privacy is a concern? +
Yes — the Blinds Between Glass option is specifically designed for entries where privacy control matters. The blinds can be fully lowered for complete privacy or fully raised for an open view, and every setting in between is available via the tilt function. A full-lite entry door without any diffusion or blinds is exposed to the street when the interior is lit — address this before choosing the clear glass option if your entry faces directly toward a public space.
