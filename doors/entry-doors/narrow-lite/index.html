The Narrow Lite is built for entries where you want light but not a clear view in. The slim vertical glass insert admits daylight and creates a visual connection between entry and exterior without the wide-open exposure of a full-lite or 3/4 lite design. The Materials Behind the Glass

01

THE NARROW LITE INSERT

Light In. Street View Out.

The narrow vertical glass insert is the defining feature of this door — it admits natural light into the entry from a slim, column-like opening rather than a wide glass area. The insert is tall and narrow, giving the door a clean visual line without creating a clear view through the entry from the street. It's the right choice when the entry faces the street directly, when sightlines into the home are a concern, or when a full or 3/4 lite design feels too open for the placement. GLASS OPTIONS

Three Privacy Options

Water Wave — rippled texture glass, diffuses light and blurs the view without completely blocking it. Some visual connection between interior and exterior but no clear sightline. Radiant — [VERIFY: confirm Radiant glass description — pattern type, privacy level, and light transmission characteristics from Home Depot listing before publishing.] Frosted — uniform matte-white surface, maximum privacy, full light transmission with no exterior view. Choose Frosted for entries that face the street directly or where interior privacy is the primary concern.

03

FINISH OPTIONS

White and Black — Both Directions

White — the classic neutral; pairs with virtually any exterior siding color. Reads as clean and minimal, which suits the narrow lite's contemporary profile. Black — bold, high contrast; works with gray, white, blue, green, or natural wood siding. Black entry doors are the most replicated exterior renovation choice in modern residential design for a reason — the contrast reads as intentional from the street. Both are factory applied to composite substrate — no chipping from steel expansion, no rot from wood moisture absorption.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a "narrow lite" — how much glass is on the door? +
A narrow lite is a slim, vertically oriented glass insert set into the door panel — it is a much smaller glass area than a full lite (full panel) or a 3/4 lite (upper three-quarters of the panel). The insert is tall and narrow, typically positioned vertically near the center or edge of the door. The result is a door that reads mostly as a solid panel from the street, with a single vertical glass detail that admits light without creating a wide view through the entry. [VERIFY: confirm insert dimensions and exact position from Home Depot listing before publishing.]
How does the Narrow Lite compare to a full-lite or 3/4 lite door for privacy? +
The Narrow Lite provides more privacy than a full-lite or 3/4 lite door for two reasons: the glass area is much smaller, and all three glass options (Water Wave, Radiant, Frosted) diffuse the view rather than transmitting it clearly. A full-lite door with clear glass provides a nearly unobstructed view through the entry. A 3/4 lite door with decorative glass provides moderate privacy depending on the glass pattern. The Narrow Lite with any of its three glass options provides maximum privacy — a narrow, diffused glass column that lets light in without letting the street see in.
