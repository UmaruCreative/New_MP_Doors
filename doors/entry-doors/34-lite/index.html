What does "3/4 lite" mean — how much glass is on the door? +
A 3/4 lite entry door has decorative glass covering the upper three-quarters of the door panel. The bottom quarter is solid composite panel. This proportion is one of the most common residential entry door designs — it brings daylight into the entry without a full-glass exposure, and the solid lower section gives the door visual grounding and structural mass. If you want maximum light, the full lite is the right choice. If you want light with more privacy and visual weight at the base, the 3/4 lite is the standard choice.

How does the 3/4 Lite compare to the Oval Lite doors? +
The 3/4 Lite and the Oval Lite doors both cover the upper three-quarters of the door with glass, but the glass area is shaped differently. The Oval Lite uses an oval-shaped glass cutout — the oval is set within a larger solid panel surround, giving the glass a framed, decorative appearance. The 3/4 Lite uses a full-width glass panel across the upper three-quarters — the glass runs edge to edge across the door width, creating a larger, more open glass area. The 3/4 Lite reads as more contemporary; the Oval Lite reads as more traditional and decorative. Both are composite construction with the same MasterPiece Series warranty structure.
Does the composite frame require any maintenance? +
No ongoing maintenance is required from the composite frame. Composite doesn't absorb moisture, so it doesn't rot, warp, or require repainting because of seasonal weather cycling. The factory finish is applied to composite substrate — unlike wood, where the substrate beneath the finish expands and contracts with moisture, eventually driving the finish to crack.
