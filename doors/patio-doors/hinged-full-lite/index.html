The Materials Behind the Glass

01

GLASS DESIGN

Full Glass, Top to Bottom

Full-lite means the entire door panel is glass — no solid rail splitting the view. You choose how that glass looks and performs: standard Low-E for maximum clarity and thermal control, Blinds Between Glass for privacy on demand, or four grille patterns (Grilles Between Glass, Simulated Divided Lites, 10-Lite, and 15-Lite) for a more traditional divided-light appearance.

02

FRAME CONSTRUCTION

Composite Frame, No Seasonal Drama

The frame is composite — the material does not absorb moisture, so it holds its shape in Florida humidity the same way it does in Minnesota winters. A door that doesn't move with the seasons is a door whose hardware stays aligned and whose weatherstrip seals correctly year after year. Frequently Asked Questions

Answers verified before publishing — [VERIFY] items noted throughout

What's the difference between the Hinged Full Lite and the Full Lite Gliding? +
Both doors are full glass — the entire panel is glass rather than a partial insert. The difference is operation. The Full Lite Gliding slides on a track: one panel is fixed, the other slides open. The Hinged Full Lite swings open on hinges like a traditional door. No track means no track to clean or maintain, and the door closes with a latching mechanism rather than a sliding latch. If you want a patio opening that operates like a standard door, the hinged option is the right choice. If you have limited swing clearance, the gliding door is the better fit.
What glass option should I choose for privacy on a street-facing patio? +
The Blinds Between Glass option is the most practical choice for a street-facing application. The blinds are enclosed between the glass panes — not on the interior where they collect dust — and tilt open or closed independently of the door. When open, you have a clear full-glass view. When closed, you have full privacy without adding any window treatment. The Simulated Divided Lites or grille options add visual texture but do not provide privacy. Standard Low-E is fully transparent. What handing options are available — and what does "handing" mean? +
Handing describes which side the hinges are on and which direction the door swings. Stand outside the opening facing the door: if the hinges are on your right, the door is right-handed. If they're on your left, it's left-handed. Inswing means the door swings into the home; outswing means it swings toward the exterior. Before ordering, confirm the swing clearance — outswing requires clear space on the exterior; inswing requires clear interior floor space matching the door's sweep radius. What is the Blinds Between Glass option, and how does it work? +
Blinds Between Glass means the blinds are installed inside the sealed glass unit — between the two panes — rather than as a surface-mounted window treatment. This keeps the blinds out of reach of pets and children, and eliminates dust accumulation because the blinds are sealed from the room's air.
