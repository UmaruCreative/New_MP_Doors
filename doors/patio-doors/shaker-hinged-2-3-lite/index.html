What Makes It Different

01

BOTH PANELS OPERABLE

No Fixed Panel. No Center Post.

On most double hinged patio doors, one panel is fixed in place and the other swings. The Shaker Hinged 2/3 Lite is built differently: both the active panel and the passive panel are fully operable. Open both and you get a clear, unobstructed opening — no structural post in the center interrupting the pass-through. The passive panel's flush bolt locks engage at the top and bottom frame when closed, so the door seals and stays stable without a fixed post.

02

CONSTRUCTION

Fiberglass Panels. Full Composite Frame. 100% Waterproof.

The panels are fiberglass — confirmed on the manufacturer's product page. The frame is full composite, 100% waterproof, with complete composite edging. It resists rotting, warping, splitting, delaminating, denting, and rusting. The door ships double-bored — the hardware pre-positions are factory-set, reducing install complexity. A patented water-resistant draining system is included, along with weather-stripping insulation.

The Blinds Between Glass option seals the blinds inside the glass unit — no surface-mounted blinds to collect dust or get caught by kids and pets. Frequently Asked Questions

Answers based on confirmed product page data — [VERIFY] items noted

Can both panels actually open — or is one fixed? +
Both panels open. The active panel operates on hinges as a standard swing door. The passive panel uses two flush bolt locks — one engaging into the top frame, one into the bottom — to stay closed when needed. When you retract the flush bolts and open both panels, there's no center post in the opening. This is the key difference between this door and most double hinged patio doors, where one panel is permanently fixed. Open both panels and the full 72" width of the opening is clear.
What is the 2/3 lite format — how much of the door is glass? +
2/3 lite means glass occupies the upper two-thirds of each panel. The lower one-third is a solid composite panel. On a 72" × 80" double door, you have two 36"-wide panels each with approximately 53" of glass from the top and a solid 27" composite rail at the bottom. This is a middle ground between a full lite door (all glass, top to bottom) and the 3/4 lite (more glass, smaller solid bottom rail). The 2/3 lite solid bottom section is larger — more suited to homes where pets or children interact regularly with the lower door surface, or where a more substantial lower panel matches the Shaker style profile better.
What does "double-bored" mean — and what hardware is included? +
Double-bored means the door panel has two pre-drilled openings — one for the deadbolt and one for the lockset — positioned at standard heights. The pre-drilling is done at the factory, so you're not drilling into the composite panel on site. What is the patented water-resistant draining system — and what does it prevent? +
The water-resistant draining system is integrated into the door's threshold area to manage water that enters the base of the door opening — typically from rain driven under the door or pooling at the threshold. It channels that water away rather than letting it infiltrate the interior or sit against the frame. Composite construction doesn't absorb water the way wood does, but water at the threshold still presents a risk of interior moisture and finish damage if it isn't managed. [VERIFY: confirm the mechanism of the draining system from MP Doors technical documentation before publishing a more detailed description.]
Is the Blinds Between Glass option worth the price difference? +
The Blinds Between Glass option seals the blinds inside the insulated glass unit — between the two panes. They're completely inaccessible from the room side, which means they accumulate no dust and can't be bent, broken, or moved by pets or children. On a patio door that opens to an outdoor space, blinds that are never handled are blinds that are never replaced. Whether the price premium makes sense depends on how much you'd otherwise spend on replacement treatments over time, and how much you value zero maintenance on that window covering. The Low-E option is correct if you don't need privacy control at the door — it costs less and delivers the same glass performance. The pet door is available as an add-on at the time of purchase — it's built into the solid lower third of one panel at the factory. Retrofitting a pet door into a composite fiberglass panel after delivery is generally not recommended without manufacturer guidance, as it may affect warranty coverage and structural integrity of the panel.
