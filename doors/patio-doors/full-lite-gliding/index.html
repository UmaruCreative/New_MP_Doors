The Materials Behind the Glass

01

GLASS OPTIONS

Six Ways to Configure the Glass

Low-E Glass — the standard option. Low-Emissivity coating manages heat transfer in both directions, keeping interiors cooler in summer and warmer in winter. Blinds Between Glass — built-in blinds between the glass panes, operated without opening the door; no cleaning, no tangling. Grilles Between Glass (GBG) — a divided-light look without the cleaning surface of traditional divided lites. Simulated Divided Lites (SDL) — applied grilles on the glass surface for a traditional divided-lite appearance. 10-Lite Grill and 15-Lite Grill — specific grille patterns for defined traditional aesthetics.

What is the difference between PG45 and PG50 — which one do I need? +
Performance Grade (PG) is a structural rating that tells you the loads the door system has been tested to withstand. PG45 is the standard grade and meets requirements for most residential applications. PG50 is the higher structural grade — you may need it if your local building code requires it, your home is in a climate with significant wind loads, or your contractor specifies it for a particular installation. If you are not sure which grade your project requires, check your local building code or ask your installer — the required grade is a code question, not a preference question. [VERIFY: confirm specific test loads for PG45 and PG50 on this door system from product spec before publishing.]
What are the glass options and which one is right for my situation? +
Low-E Glass is the standard — best thermal performance, clear view, no visual obstruction. Choose this if energy efficiency is the priority. Blinds Between Glass suits rooms where you want privacy control without cleaning between the glass — the blinds are sealed inside the unit. Grilles Between Glass (GBG) gives a traditional divided-light look with the maintenance benefit of sealed grilles. Simulated Divided Lites (SDL) are applied on the glass surface for a traditional appearance at lower cost than GBG. 10-Lite and 15-Lite Grills are specific grid patterns suited to craftsman or traditional architectural styles. How does the Full Lite Gliding compare to the Hinged Full Lite Patio Door? +
Both are full-lite patio doors in composite construction with the same MasterPiece Series warranty structure. The key difference is how they open. The Gliding door slides along a track — no floor space is needed on either side because the panel moves parallel to the wall. The Hinged door swings open on a hinge — it needs clear floor space on the swing side but provides a wider, unobstructed opening when fully open and typically seals more tightly when closed. Choose Gliding when floor space in the room is tight or when you want a classic sliding door operation. Choose Hinged when you want maximum opening width and a swing-door operation.
