Common Questions

Answers before you need them.

What is the difference between composite and vinyl patio doors? +
Composite construction resists the failure modes that end vinyl doors early — warping under heat, surface degradation from UV, and moisture intrusion at joints. A composite door holds its geometry and finish without the maintenance cycle vinyl requires.
Is MP Doors Energy Star certified for my climate? +
Yes. Every MP Doors product is Energy Star certified across all four U.S. climate zones — Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern. One certification that covers Minnesota winters and Gulf Coast summers alike.
What does the warranty actually cover? +
Structural components — frame, panels, and astragals — carry a Limited Lifetime Warranty for the original purchaser in an owner-occupied home. Locking mechanism: 10 years. Glass seal: 20 years, prorated. Full terms are on the Warranty & Support page.
Can I install an MP Doors door myself? +
Professional installation is recommended. Doors require precise leveling, proper flashing, and structural anchoring. Installation quality directly determines long-term performance. The Home Depot offers professional installation services.
What do I do if my door arrives damaged? +
Inspect before the installer leaves. Call 1-888-366-7717 within 5 calendar days to report delivery damage — do not wait. After that window, delivery damage is no longer eligible for coverage.
Where can I buy MP Doors products? +
MP Doors products are available in store and online through one retailer. Use the Shop button anywhere on this site to go directly to the product listings.
